Synopsis
Spiritual leader Sadhguru suggests a new approach to life. He believes focusing on hard work leads to stress and dissatisfaction. Sadhguru advocates for studying joyfully and working lovingly. This shift, he explains, aligns mind and body for better performance. His insights have resonated with many online, highlighting the benefits of a more pleasant state of mind for success.

Sadhguru urged everyone to work joyfully for ultimate productivity. (Instagram and iStock0
Right from our childhood, we are taught that 
success
 comes only through 
hard work
, constant pressure, and relentless effort. Students are encouraged to study hard for academic achievement. This mindset can carry into professional life, where employees are expected to work tirelessly to achieve career growth and financial stability. Over time, this continuous pressure leads to stress, burnout, and dissatisfaction, turning us into complaining adults who can’t even take the slightest inconvenience. Now, 
Sadhguru
 has shared his knowledge on the matter in a video on Instagram. 
Sadhguru on hard work
In the clip, Sadhguru talked about how people are overly-focused on doing everything the ‘hard’ way. The spiritual leader revealed that parents urge their children to study hard for good scores. Similarly, professionals often push themselves to the limit by working hard, trying to prove their worth and elevate their workplace value. This pattern continues across generations without anyone seeing any problem with it.

According to Sadhguru, this ‘hard work’ mindset, when applied forcefully, makes us complain about everything in life, regardless of the matter. He urged everyone to shift their thinking for ultimate 
productivity
 and success. Sadhguru advised individuals to ‘study joyfully’ and ‘work lovingly’, because until you love your work, nothing good can come out of it.

Explaining the concept further through substantial medical and scientific evidence, Sadhguru shared the importance of your body and mind being aligned. He pointed out that only when we are in a pleasant and content state of mind, our work activities improve and we tend to perform better at whatever we are doing — be it preparing for an examination in school or delivering a pitch-perfect presentation at work.

A post shared by Sadhguru (@sadhguru)
Internet reactions and more about Sadhguru
Sadhguru’s video resonated with many on the internet. ‘This has been true in my experience. I am at my best when I am relaxed and joyful,’ noted a user. Echoing a similar sentiment, another wrote, ‘A generation rooted in survival sees everything as hard. What they endured, others also must.’ Meanwhile, an individual admired Sadhguru’s ‘wise words’.

Sadhguru is the founder of the Isha Foundation, which he established in 1992 in Coimbatore. The organisation focuses on yoga, meditation, and programs aimed at improving inner well-being. He has also written several books, like Inner Engineering: A Yogi’s Guide to Joy and Karma: A Yogi’s Guide to Crafting Your Destiny, where he talks about personal growth and awareness.
