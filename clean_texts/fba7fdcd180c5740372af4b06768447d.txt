                        LibreOffice
               

            You no longer need to pay for Microsoft 365
    
LibreOffice is an open-source office suite that covers the basic productivity tools most people need day to day. It includes a word processor, spreadsheet tool, presentation app, drawing tool, and database component, all in one package. On Windows, it works as a straightforward alternative for anyone who does not want to pay for Microsoft 365 to write documents, manage spreadsheets, or create slides.
The best part about LibreOffice is that it handles the core office workflow without forcing a subscription model. You can open, edit, and save common file formats such as DOCX, XLSX, and PPTX, which makes it practical for people who still work with Microsoft Office files. It also supports exporting to PDF.
LibreOffice is enough for most of us because the common tasks are simple and don't require a lot. You can write reports, format resumes, create budgets, build basic charts, and make presentations without needing more expensive software. The interface is familiar enough once you spend a little time with it, and the suite is stable for everyday work.

                        Kdenlive
               

            An almost perfect replacement for Premiere Pro
    
Kdenlive is a non-linear video editor built for those who need more than a basic trimming tool but do not want to pay for a premium video editor. It’s commonly used for YouTube videos, tutorials, interviews, social clips, and general content editing. On Windows, it gives me a serious editing workflow without locking me into a monthly subscription (Hi Adobe).
The app supports multi-track editing, timeline-based cuts, transitions, audio mixing, and a range of visual effects. You can layer clips, add titles, adjust audio, and manage longer edits the same way you would on a tool like Premiere Pro.
Kdenlive also supports a broad range of video and audio formats, which matters when you are moving footage from phones, cameras, screen recordings, or downloaded clips. It is not built around a stripped-down beginner mode, so it can handle more demanding projects once you learn the workflow. It’s also local software that you control yourself.

                        GIMP
               

            Bye bye, Photoshop
    
✕

GIMP is an open-source image editor
 you can use for photo editing, compositing, retouching, and general graphic work. It’s a solid alternative to Adobe Photoshop on Windows. The app is useful for tasks like resizing images, removing backgrounds, adjusting colors, cleaning up photos, creating web graphics, and preparing thumbnails or banners.
It also supports layers, masks, selection tools, and plugins, which gives it enough depth for more advanced editing work. For people who are comfortable learning a slightly different workflow, GIMP can cover a lot of common design and image manipulation needs.
It's worth noting that GIMP is not an exact shop clone and doesn't even try to be one. The interfaces and shortcuts are very different, so there is a bit of a learning curve. It’s not an exact Photoshop clone, and it does not try to be one. GIMP is especially useful for people who edit images for blogs, social boards, YouTube thumbnails, documentation, or simple marketing assets that could be printable or something else.

                        Bitwarden
               

            You don't need to pay for 1Password
    
✕
 Remove Ads
Bitwarden is a 1Password alternative
 that lets you store login details, secure notes, payment information, and other sensitive data in an encrypted vault. It works across desktop, mobile, and browser extensions, which makes it useful if you use more than one device or switch between browsers during the day.
The main value of Bitwarden is convenience without giving up security. Instead of remembering dozens of passwords, you keep them in one place and unlock the vault with a master password or another supported method. It can generate strong passwords, autofill logins, and sync your vault across devices. But if that is not enough, you can also self-host Bitwarden on your own hardware. It could be your PC, a NAS, or any other device you have handy. That gives you complete control over some of the most important information in your life.

                        Joplin
               

            It's actually better than Notion
    
 Joplin is my 
favorite note-taking app
, and I consider it much better than Notion. I self-host Joplin on my NAS because I think my notes are some of the most valuable digital information I have. My life practically lives in my notes, so I want full control over them instead of trusting someone else’s cloud. Even as a self-hosted app, Joplin is perfectly capable and offers almost everything you would expect from a modern note-taking app, including most of the features you would get with something like Notion. The only thing largely missing is the AI slop, although even that can be added through extensions.

The app supports Markdown, which makes it attractive to people who like clean, plain-text formatting and portable notes. You can structure notes with headings, lists, links, code blocks, and attachments without relying on a complicated online editor.
It works well for personal organization, research, and everyday productivity. The app is not trying to replace a full project management suite, but it is very capable of a note database that you can search, organize, and revisit later.
