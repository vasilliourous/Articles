Some people treat self-hosted apps as permanent infrastructure. Once installed, they leave them running forever, waiting to be used, even if in reality many apps are only actually needed for a few hours a week.
✕
 Remove Ads
Always-on uptime is necessary for things that actually need it, such as smart home services and networking tools, but there are plenty of apps that you can run on a schedule. Using options such as cron jobs or GUI-based schedulers, apps can run only when you actually need them.

                        Calibre-Web
               

            You don't need a new book at 4 AM
    
Credit: Patrick Campanale / How-To Geek
Calibre-Web
 is one of those self-hosted apps that you read about, set up, use twice, and then often forget about. The temptation with self-hosting is to install all the things, leaving you with apps that run unused for weeks at a time.
I struggle to fit in half an hour of reading in bed at night before I fall asleep, so a lengthy book can take me a while to get through. While I'm reading my current book, Calibre-Web is going completely untouched, but keeps chugging away in the background anyway.
✕
 Remove Ads
Aside from the unnecessary waste of resources running a service you're not actively using, security vulnerabilities have been found in Calibre-Web in the past. Running Calibre-Web on a more limited schedule can reduce the 
attack surface
, especially if it's exposed for 
remote Kindle uploads
.

                        Jellyfin
               

            Schedule your media server around your watching habits
    
Credit: Lucas Gouveia/How-To Geek | Kaspars Grinvalds/Shutterstock
 At first, running 
Jellyfin
 at specific times sounds like a crazy idea. The whole point is to have access to content that you can watch at any time. If Jellyfin isn't running, you can't access your media.
It's not as crazy as it sounds, however. If you work all day, you're not going to be using your 
Jellyfin server
 from 9 to 5. It's unlikely that you'll need it in the middle of the night either. You can set it up so that Jellyfin is only running at the times you actually need it.
✕
 Remove Ads
The question is: why bother? The most obvious reason is the cost. Jellyfin is often run on hardware that consumes a reasonable amount of electricity, especially if you're using multiple hard drives or a high-TDP processor. While you're not going to notice your energy bills plummeting if you run Jellyfin on a schedule, those savings can add up over the course of a year.
If Jellyfin is exposed outside your home network, it's also another attack surface for exploits, and the less it's up, the smaller your window of vulnerability. Another reason to run it on a schedule is that having some dedicated "media off" time could be 
beneficial for your mental health
.

                        Paperless-ngx
               

            Ingestion is sometimes a batch job
    
Credit: Paperless-ngx
✕
 Remove Ads
Paperless-ngx
 is a great app for 
digitizing your documents
 so that you don't have to hold on to mounds of paperwork. For some, ingestion can happen through watched folders and incoming emails, but if you tend to upload documents manually in batches, then you really don't need Paperless-ngx to be running all the time. If you set aside time on a Sunday afternoon to upload the documents from the week, then having the service running Monday through Saturday is major overkill.
Paperless-ngx feels like it should be an "always-on" app. However, you may find that the way you use it means that it rarely needs to be on at all.

                        Nextcloud
               

            Sync clients can be patient
    
This is probably the point where most people are going to raise their eyebrows. 
Nextcloud
 is a 
self-hosted cloud storage service
, and the entire point is that it lets you access your files whenever you need them. If any app feels like it should be running 24/7, it's Nextcloud.
✕
 Remove Ads
If you're using Nextcloud for personal use, however, then there may be long periods where you never upload or access files. For some people, while instant synchronization is nice to have, it's not something that's vital; Nextcloud's sync client on desktop can handle server unavailability, queuing changes locally, and syncing when the server comes back online.
You may not use Nextcloud during your workday and almost certainly won't use it when you're asleep. Nextcloud often runs on hardware that's more capable than it needs to be, and if you're not using it, turning it off during the day is an easy win. You only need it to be running when you need to use it, and keeping the server offline reduces the risk of opportunistic scans, login attacks, or exposure to security vulnerabilities.
If you use Nextcloud constantly, running on a schedule is not a good idea. For some people, however, their personal use may mean that it's not a service that needs to run all the time.

                        Duplicati
               

            Backups happen on a schedule
    
✕
 Remove Ads
Taking backups
 is one of the golden rules of any good homelab. If you're not using a proper 
3-2-1 backup system
, you could be at risk of losing important data. Backups are almost always run on a schedule, so you don't necessarily need your backup software to be running outside these times.

If you always take a backup at 3 AM, for example, then having a backup app such as 
Duplicati
 running all day and night is pointless. For the rest of the time, it's doing nothing but using up resources for no good reason.
Backup apps are the perfect candidates for running on a schedule because you know exactly when you need them and when you don't. Fire them up when you want to take your backup and shut them down again when they're done.

            24/7 uptime is important, but not for every app
    
Some of your homelab apps need to run 24/7. You don't want your house to flood because you stopped 
Home Assistant
 running for the night, and your 
water leak automation
 didn't run. You may have very solid reasons for needing 24/7 access to some of the apps listed above, too.
✕
 Remove Ads
The likelihood is, however, that there are some apps in your homelab that spend the vast majority of their time doing nothing at all. If this is the case, give them a rest and only run them when you need them.
