analogous \ əˈnæləgəs \ adjective
1. 
similar or equivalent in some respects
2. 
corresponding in function but not in evolutionary origin

The word 
analogous
 has appeared in 54 articles on NYTimes.com in the past year, including on Nov. 24 in “
Living, Breathing, Seeing and Teaching Theater
,” by Elisabeth Vincentelli. It includes statements by James Bundy, the artistic director of Yale Repertory Theater:
“One way of thinking about how our theater operates is that we’re 
analogous
 to a teaching hospital and its relationship to a medical school,” said Bundy, whose own revival of Ibsen’s “Hedda Gabler” is set to begin on Friday and runs through Dec. 20. “So the people who are studying end up involved in aspects of the production. For instance, all the designers of ‘Hedda Gabler’ are third-year M.F.A. candidates in set design, costume design, lighting design and sound design.”
