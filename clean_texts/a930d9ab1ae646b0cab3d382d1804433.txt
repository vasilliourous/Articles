Cloud Complexity: How To Simplify Without Sacrificing Speed
By
Expert Panel®
,
Forbes Councils Member.
for 
Forbes Technology Council

Expertise from Forbes Councils members, operated under license. Opinions expressed are those of the author.
 | Membership (fee-based)
Apr 20, 2026, 01:15pm EDT
getty
Cloud-native and multicloud environments give technology teams more flexibility in how they build, deploy and scale systems. But that added flexibility can also create more moving parts, making it harder to maintain visibility, enforce consistent standards and keep operations manageable as environments grow.
For teams trying to move quickly, the challenge is finding ways to reduce operational friction without creating new bottlenecks or limiting innovation. Below, members of 
Forbes Technology Council
 share their perspectives on how technology teams can keep complexity in check while continuing to innovate in cloud-native and multicloud environments.
Standardize On A Unified Platform Layer
Standardize on a platform layer using a unified hybrid cloud platform. Consistent engineering processes with self-service tools and pipelines allow dev teams to adopt AI and innovate without reinventing infrastructure. Embed telemetry and policy-as-code across environments from day one so observability and governance scale with you, not against you. -
 Brian Gruttadauria
,
 HPE
PROMOTED
Use ML And Automation To Enforce Guardrails
Too many people try to manage complexity with dashboards. That’s like trying to fight a wildfire with a spreadsheet. Drive simplicity through ML and automation instead. ML makes sense of the complex, and automation scales it. Keep engineers focused on shipping fast. Let machines enforce discipline, because speed without guardrails is chaos. And a guardrail without automation is just bureaucracy. -
 Yasmin Rajabi
,
 CloudBolt
Forbes Technology Council
 is an invitation-only community for world-class CIOs, CTOs and technology executives. 
Do I qualify?
Start Simple With Infrastructure As Code
Start simple and don’t overengineer—especially if you are a startup! Adopting infrastructure as code and, of course, different environments will help with rapid prototyping and experimentation. -
 Leonardo Felipe Nerone
,
 Drachma
Build Self-Service Platforms With Embedded Guardrails
Strategic focus on automation reduces complexity in multicloud environments. For instance, platform engineering teams create preapproved tools and templates for developer self-service. These are further used by AI assistants to apply security and governance standards, allowing developers to bypass infrastructure bottlenecks and push code speedily while maintaining architectural consistency. -
 Amrit Jassal
,
 Egnyte
Abstract Infrastructure With Distributed Databases And Open APIs
A multicloud distributed architecture helps survive cloud outages and optimize spend. A key strategy is to adopt distributed databases and standardize on an open API like PostgreSQL to abstract away infrastructure complexity. Built-in high availability, seamless failover and multiregion consistency reduce operational burden, enabling faster innovation without compromising uptime or performance. -
 Karthik Ranganathan
,
 Yugabyte

Stabilize Critical Systems Before Upgrading
Much of the complexity in multicloud environments is self-inflicted through constant upgrade cycles. Technology teams should challenge the assumption that newer always means better. By stabilizing critical systems and upgrading only when there is real value, teams reduce disruption and free up time to focus on innovation. -
 Tomas O Leary
,
 Origina
Centralize Complexity With Platform Engineering
Adopt a platform engineering approach. Build standardized internal developer platforms with reusable cloud-native templates, guardrails and automated policies. This reduces multicloud complexity while enabling teams to innovate quickly on a consistent, secure foundation without reinventing infrastructure for every project. -
 Sandeep Telu
,
 Infosys Consulting
Create Shared Architectural Foundations
My perspective is that investing in the conceptual foundation is more critical than ever. This is what allows velocity to persist, even when environments scale to multicloud, multiregion setups. Operational complexity compounds at the decision-making level even more so than at the infrastructure level. When teams lack shared primitives, every architectural choice turns into a negotiation. -
 James Theuerkauf
,
 Anaplan
Separate Exploration, Build And Operations Roles
Separate your explorers from your builders and your operators—and deploy them in the right order. Explorers experiment and find what’s possible. Builders productize it. Operators make it stable and secure. Most teams fail because everyone plays the same role simultaneously. A healthy tension between the three is what ships things without burning everything down. -
 Paul Deraval
,
 NinjaCat
Treat Internal Platforms Like Products
Gartner predicts 
80%
 of organizations will adopt platform engineering by 2026. My strategy: Treat your internal developer platform as a product, not a project. Build golden paths and preapproved templates so developers get self-service infrastructure in minutes. Obsess over developer experience; don’t mandate adoption. Complexity doesn’t disappear; it just lives in one place. -
 Hastimal Jangid
,
 Coozmoo Digital Solutions, Inc.
Unify Cloud And On-Premises Operations
Treating cloud environments as separate silos increases friction and complexity. Instead, teams should adopt a unified approach that manages on-premises and cloud together. This reduces operational overhead, improves consistency and enables innovation without introducing delays or fragmentation. -
 Erez Tadmor
,
 Tufin
Adopt A Service Catalog Operating Model
Adopting a product and platform engineering mindset with an internal service catalog helps teams manage cloud inventory, configs and self-service IaC workflows. Standardizing tools, architecture and deployment models with service mesh for microservices and FinOps practices reduces operational complexity and improves governance across multicloud environments while supporting faster innovation. -
 Ashish Anand
,
 Marriott International
Reduce Cognitive Load With Self-Service IDPs
Adopt a platform engineering approach with internal developer platforms. By standardizing infrastructure, tooling and workflows behind self-service interfaces, teams reduce cognitive load and inconsistency while enabling fast, safe deployments. This balances control with flexibility, letting developers innovate without managing underlying complexity. -
 Dax Grant
,
 Global Transform
Turn Governance Into Code
Cloud-native and multicloud environments enable agility and resilience—complexity is the price of both. Stop treating operations as a manual checklist. A modern engineering ecosystem—CI/CD, GitOps, continuous testing and telemetry—transforms governance into observable code. Teams enforce rigor without sacrificing velocity. Without it, you’re navigating a complexity storm half-blind. -
 Song Bac Toh
,
 Dell Technologies
Use A Federated Model With Centralized Governance 
Most teams tackle multicloud complexity with tooling, but the real bottleneck is the operating model. A federated architecture with centralized governance lets a core team set standards and guardrails, while cloud-specific teams innovate on a “paved road,” cutting duplication and scaling innovation. -
 Girish Joshi
,
 Collabera
Standardize Interfaces And Integration Patterns
Control complexity by standardizing and simplifying interfaces. In multicloud environments, complexity often comes from inconsistent APIs, contracts and integration patterns. By enforcing clear interface boundaries, teams can change underlying services freely while keeping interactions predictable, reducing confusion without slowing innovation. -
 Yogesh Malik
, Way2Direct
Add A Connective Tissue Layer For Live Data
By implementing connective tissue layers, like a model context protocol (MCP) server, IT teams can link live data with agents to avoid costly rebuilds that slow down innovation. By consolidating infrastructures and shifting priorities toward real-time data for visibility, organizations can control costs and complexity across clouds without sacrificing agility due to legacy systems. -
 Guillaume Aymé
,
 Lenses.io
Use Microservices To Isolate And Scale Domains
Microservices can reduce complexity by letting teams isolate domains, develop and deploy independently, and scale parts of a system without changing everything else. The team has to adapt a specific programming protocol, and the rest is easy with managed orchestrators. -
 Abdallah Chalhoub
,
 ZeroGPT
Prioritize Consistency And Cross-Cloud Visibility
The best way to manage multicloud complexity is to focus on consistency. Establishing shared governance principles and unified visibility across environments helps teams maintain control while still giving developers the freedom to move quickly and innovate. -
 Abhi Sharma
,
 Relyance AI
Apply SRE Principles Across Operations
Adopt site reliability engineering principles to standardize operations across clouds. By defining common SLIs, SLOs and observability patterns, teams can manage complexity from both infrastructure and business processes. When reliability metrics guide operations, technology teams innovate within clear guardrails while keeping multicloud environments stable and predictable. -
 Nirab Kumar
,
 Odyssey Logistics
