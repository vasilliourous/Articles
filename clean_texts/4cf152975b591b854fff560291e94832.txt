If you created your Gmail account with an email address you don’t want anymore, this is your big day. Google is finally rolling out the ability to change your primary Gmail address.
Google announced today that all accounts in the United States should be able to change their usernames. You can do it by 
visiting your Google account email settings
, clicking ‘Personal info’ at the top-left corner, then Email > Google Account email > Change Google Account email. Google 
started testing the feature
 in December 2025, but it was only formally revealed today.
✕
 Remove Ads
Google still supports 
using a third-party email address
 as an alternative login method, so if you have 
example@gmail.com
 and 
example@outlook.com
, you can add the Outlook email and sign in with that. You can also 
delete your Gmail entirely
, which will require using a third-party email address as your sign-in method.
There are a few potential issues with changing your primary Gmail address. If you have a Chromebook, you might need to remove your local account before changing, then sign in again after the switch. That’s going to be annoying if you have Android or Linux apps that can’t be easily backed up. A support page explained, “This is necessary so that your Chromebook will update to use your new Google Account email. Otherwise after you log in your home directory will sometimes appear empty.”
When you change your primary Gmail address, your old email is saved as an alternative email address, and you will continue to receive emails for that address. You can still delete the old one if you want—it just doesn’t happen automatically.
