Most developers think they’re not productive because they don’t have enough time.
“I need more hours.”
 “I need fewer meetings.”
 “I need less work.”
But after working with different teams and systems, I realized something surprising:
Time is rarely the real problem. Workflow is.
And fixing it changes everything.
Press enter or click to view image in full size
The Invisible Problem Slowing You Down
A typical developer day doesn’t look like coding.
It looks like this:
You start working →
 You get stuck →
 You search for something →
 You switch context →
 You come back →
 You forget what you were doing →
 Repeat.
Nothing feels broken.
But everything feels slow.
The Real Bottleneck: Context Switching
Every time you switch tasks, your brain resets.
You lose:
focus
momentum
understanding
Even small interruptions cost more than you think.
And most workflows are full of them.
What High-Productivity Developers Do Differently
They don’t work more hours.
They reduce friction.
Their workflow is designed to:
minimize interruptions
reduce decision fatigue
keep momentum going
That’s the difference.
Step 1: Remove “Start Friction”
Starting is often the hardest part.
Opening files.
 Setting up context.
 Remembering what you were doing.
