Gemini understands that users might want more export options, and that's exactly what it's giving users now. 
In a new blog post
, the tech giant announced that it is now going to be easier to turn your research into ready-to-share files. 
Credit: Google
Gemini can now export your research into PDFs, Microsoft Word and Excel files, Google Docs, Sheets, Slides, .xlsx, .csv, LaTeX, Plain Text (TXT), Rich Text Format (RTF), and Markdown (MD) files. 
This essentially removes the need for copying, pasting and reformatting.
It's worth noting that there's no new dedicated 'export' button. You simply prompt Gemini about the format you want to export your research in, and the AI tool does the rest.
Export your budget proposal to a Microsoft Excel (.xlsx) file, arrange loose ideas into a bulleted draft or consolidate a lengthy collaboration into a single-page PDF or Microsoft Word (.docx). For most formats, you can then directly download the file to your device or export to Drive.
The new export options are now available to all Gemini app users globally. I have acess to the feature on Gemini on the web as well.
