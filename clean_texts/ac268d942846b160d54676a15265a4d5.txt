I'm always looking to make things easier for myself. Call this laziness. Call this drive. Why would I work harder when I can work smarter? In all seriousness, I have really enjoyed setting up my smart home because of all the shortcuts and automations that it presents.
✕
 Remove Ads
Being able to walk up the stairs and ask my smart speaker to turn on the lights so they are already on by the time I get there is a simple yet effective smart home feature I use every day. But I started to think about how to improve other parts of my daily life by relying on technology. That's how I discovered Near Field Communication (NFC) tags. NFC technology is not something I am unfamiliar with, as I use 
Brick
 each day to help myself lock in when I'm writing. But getting cheap tags opened up a lot of possibilities for my home that I hadn't realized.

                        Get the dance party started
               

            I can open up multiple apps
    
Credit: Chris Hachey / MakeUseOf
I have an iPhone, so I use my NFC tags with the Shortcuts app to set up automations. You can do this by opening up the app, selecting Automation at the bottom, and then choosing how you want to set up your automation from there. The NFC tag is used as a trigger and an If in many If/Then automations.
NFC tags work with an Android or iPhone, as the phone generates a magnetic field, which triggers the NFC tag and lets it send data to the phone. They work as a bridge between a phone and some kind of digital piece of information, such as a website, image, or app, similar to 
how a QR code works
.
Setting up an NFC tag is simple: bring it near a reader to set up the automation in the Shortcuts app on an iPhone and the NFC Tools app on an Android phone. I bought a pack of two NFC tags to get started with. But now that I see how much each of them can do, I'm going to be investing in more.
✕
 Remove Ads
Something that I immediately set up when I was exploring all the options was an NFC tag for the kitchen. My family and I do so much in the kitchen and it made sense to add one there. One of the things that was a must right away was using an NFC tag to open up a music app.
Scanning the tag brings up Spotify, Apple Music, or other music apps. Whichever one you choose can cause it to populate. With Apple Music, it actually lets you open it and play music right away, so you can add that and not be forced to open up the app and choose the music. This is helpful when you're cooking and need all hands on deck but still want to enjoy some quality dance time.

                        Open up a recipe while you're cooking
               

            We have a few favorites that we have saved
    
I placed an NFC tag on the top of the butter bell in our kitchen. It holds it perfectly and can be moved around the kitchen. We rarely actually use the butter, making it more likely that we won't need to flip the NFC tag over.
One of the more annoying options when you're cooking is to look up a recipe. It's frustrating because you might need to constantly tap your phone to wake it up as you're reading ingredients. You might also want to have a bigger screen than your small phone. It's part of the reason why 
my family got a Skylight Calendar
, which lets you load recipes and show them on a large 15-inch screen. 
✕
 Remove Ads
But, for anyone who wants to look up a recipe, or really any website, you can sync it to your NFC tag. We frequently use the site 
WhatsGabyCooking.com
 as it has a great array of easy-to-follow recipes.
I added that URL to the NFC tag automation and it populates the app when I want to quickly look up a recipe. Of course, you can choose a specific recipe from a site or any page that you want, and link it to the scanning of the NFC tag.

                        Start a timer on your phone
               

            You don't need to try and remember how long the pasta's been on
    
Credit: Chris Hachey / MakeUseOf
Sticking to a recipe can be challenging for some who like to ad-lib their ingredients and go by the look of the meal and their gut. My wife is one of those people and her food usually turns out excellent.
I'm not one of those people as I have to stick to a recipe or things can turn out disastrously. That's why I rely on timers to make sure I'm doing things at the right pace and parts of the dish come out at roughly the same time. The last thing you want is a warm roast chicken and cold mashed potatoes because the timing was off.
By tapping the phone on the tag, which is placed right next to my glasses, I don't need good eyesight to open the app up.
Using the NFC tag to start a timer helps me reset myself and move onto the next stage of the process without trying to throw off my entire cooking session. I'm able to put that step to the side, knowing that when the timer goes off, I'll come back and check.
✕
 Remove Ads
You can adjust the timer once it's opened, but you will need to set a certain amount of time that will open up once your NFC tag is scanned.

                        Catch up on your reading
               

            Don't forget where you were in the book
    
Credit: Chris Hachey / MakeUseOf
As mentioned above, you can listen to different music platforms by adding them as the app that opens when an NFC tag is scanned. When I'm cooking or in the kitchen, I like to listen to all kinds of music when my family is with me and podcasts when I'm cooking alone.
But, if you're a book lover, you can have the NFC tag open up an audiobook to help you stay with your story. It's a great way to keep up with your reading while your hands are tied up doing something else.
The audiobook will play upon the scan, so you'll pick up right where you left off. It's a helpful automation because it limits the amount of work you need to do manually to get your audiobook up and running.

                        Bring up the baby monitor
               

            This is a big one for me at night
    
Credit: Chris Hachey / MakeUseOf
I placed the other NFC tag that I have on my bedside table. I found that, while I'm lying in bed, being able to tap my phone on my nightstand opens up options for me.
✕
 Remove Ads
I used this for a while on my nightstand and set the Nanit app to open up when I tap it. This app is the baby monitor app that we use in our home. It opens up both my son's and my daughter's baby monitors and displays the feed on my phone.
While I can open my phone and open up the app, there isn't always time to do that. I've learned this the hard way, as my daughter might talk in her sleep and I can hear the monitor. But fumbling for my glasses and opening up the phone to see the camera feed takes a while, especially if she or my son starts to cry.
By tapping the phone on the tag, which is placed right next to my glasses, I don't need good eyesight to open the app up. It then gives me the time to put on my glasses to view the camera. While this is a small thing, a toddler yelling in the middle of the night is always going to startle me awake, so being able to see what's causing it quickly is important.

                        Turn on my smart plug
               

            It's easier to use Apple HomeKit for this
    
Credit: Chris Hachey / MakeUseOf
While I do prefer using 
Amazon Alexa and my Echo Show speakers
 as my go-to smart home ecosystem and devices, I do have some Apple HomeKit integrations I've used over the years. One of them is with a 
WeMo Smart Plug
, which I have plugged into one of the bedside lamps in our room.
✕
 Remove Ads
Rather than relying on voice commands to turn on that light, which during the night could wake up my wife or children because Alexa is quite loud when she responds, I can tap the NFC tag to turn on the plug. This is an easy way to use Apple HomeKit's integration and automations with the NFC tags.
Almost any Apple Home device can be used through the Shortcuts app with this if/then automation. You can also use this with IFTTT, Samsung SmartThings, and others with an Android phone.

                        My smart light can turn on and off
               

            This is because of the bulb I have
    
Credit: Chris Hachey / MakeUseOf
One of the other lamps in my bedroom is set up with a 
WiZ smart bulb
. I like using the WiZ bulb because it's a colored bulb and we can change the scenes in our room.

WiZ's app offers automation capabilities through Shortcuts, meaning it is one of the apps that allows you to control it through a shortcut. This gave me the opportunity to turn my smart bulb on and off by tapping the NFC tag.
Since we never use the light from our ceiling fan, I hung the NFC tag from the light switch. When I walk in the room, I can tap my phone and the light turns on. While this isn't as effective as using my smart speaker to turn it on, for anyone that doesn't have a smart speaker, it's a way to control your smart light.
It is easier than having to open up the app and access the light from there. It might even make sense to put another NFC tag going up the stairs. That way, you can tap it on your way up and the light will be on by the time you get to the room.

                        Get ready for bed with some Focus
               

            This was a no-brainer NFC tag use
    
Credit: Chris Hachey / MakeUseOf
By the end of the day, you're tired and want to go to sleep. If you're like me, you put your phone on Do Not Disturb or one of the other Focus modes the iPhone offers.
I liked having the NFC tag right on my bedside table and tapping my phone there to put it into Do Not Disturb. This means that my phone won't ring while I'm asleep, helping me avoid being woken up by a phone call or a dreaded work email in the middle of the night.
It not only helps me sleep more peacefully but also helps me set a healthier boundary from using my phone. If I don't hear it buzzing, it aids me in believing I'm not needed and I can rest. It helps quiet my brain at the end of a long day.

            NFC tags are endlessly useful
    
Not only can NFC tags do so many things, but they are ridiculously inexpensive. Deciding where to place them is the hardest part about owning them. While I listed numerous ways of using the technology, there are many more. You can help automate a smart home and help yourself get more done around the house.
