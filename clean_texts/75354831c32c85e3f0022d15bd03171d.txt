Windows 11 has a great focus mode that blocks distractions
. I've also configured my web browser to avoid distractions by adding time-wasting sites to a custom site blocker script. On top of that, I even 
replaced my task manager with a notes app
, and it was surprisingly convenient for managing tasks.
✕
 Remove Ads
All of this works, but it means I'm juggling three different apps for three different productivity needs. 
Koncentro
 is a free, open-source app that bundles a task manager, a website blocker, and a Pomodoro timer into one lightweight package and freed me from the hassle of managing multiple productivity apps. 

                        Task manager
               

            A simple to-do list that gets out of your way
    
Koncentro is available for Windows, macOS and Linux, and it's completely free and open-source. When you first launch it, the app greets you with a guided tour that walks you through each feature. It's a bit hand-holdy, but I'd say it's worth sitting through because the app has some quirks that aren't immediately obvious.
The 
To Do Tasks
 tab is where you'll spend most of your time. You can create main tasks, then nest subtasks under them. For example, I created a "Create shopping list for the week" task with subtasks like "Plan meals for the week" and "Check pantry for existing items." Each task shows an estimated time and elapsed time side by side, so you can set how long you think something will take and track how long it actually takes. If you've already started working on something before opening Koncentro, you can manually add elapsed time instead of starting from zero.
✕
 Remove Ads
Once you're done with a task, just drag and drop it from the 
To Do Tasks
 section to the 
Completed Tasks
 section below. Unlike conventional to-do apps, there are no tags, due dates, or priority flags. It's deliberately minimal, and that's sufficient for my workflow.
However, do note that during the initial setup, Koncentro asks to install MitmProxy certificates on your system. This is necessary for the website blocker to work, as it uses a local proxy to intercept and block web traffic. The app will temporarily disconnect your internet while it configures the proxy on port 8080. If you're running another proxy or VPN, this could cause conflicts. You can skip the setup if you don't need the blocker, but the app will close if you choose 
No, Setup Later
 on your first run.

                        The website blocker
               

            Block distracting sites right from the app
    
image credit - self captured (Tashreef Shareef) - No Attribution Required
 If your procrastination comes from mindlessly opening social media or streaming sites like YouTube, the most effective fix is to block them entirely. 
Browser extensions like LeechBlock are popular website blockers for Firefox and Chromium browsers
 and work well for most people. But in my experience, they're too easy to get around. You just wait out the timer, turn off the extension, or switch to a different browser.
✕
 Remove Ads
Koncentro's built-in 
Website Blocker
 tab makes it harder to bypass blocked websites. It runs a local proxy using the open-source mitmproxy tool and intercepts web traffic at the system level, not just in one browser. You can use it in two modes: a blocklist mode, where you add specific sites to block, or an allowlist mode, where only the sites you approve are accessible. Everything else gets blocked.
The blocker ties into workspaces so that you can set up different block rules for different types of work. For instance, my 
Writing 
workspace blocks social media and news sites, while my 
Research 
workspace is more relaxed. When a Pomodoro session is running in a given workspace, those blocking rules apply automatically.

                        Pomodoro timer
               

            A built-in focus timer that ties everything together
    
image credit - self captured (Tashreef Shareef) - No Attribution Required
 I've tried 
many Pomodoro apps over the years
 and even experimented with the 
trending reverse Pomodoro method
, where you take a short break first and then work for a longer stretch. It's supposed to ease you into focus mode. But I could never stick with the technique because I'd forget to start the timer, get pulled into a task for two hours straight, or just abandon the whole routine after a few days.
Koncentro's Pomodoro feature is simple yet a configurable focus session, usually 25 minutes, followed by a 5-minute break. The timer lives on its own screen, and the bottom bar always shows the current task and time remaining, so you don't lose track even when you're on the task list or blocker tab.
✕
 Remove Ads
What makes it work better for me is that the timer isn't a standalone app I have to remember to open. It's tied to my task list and my website blocker within the same window. When I start a Pomodoro, my distracting sites get blocked automatically, and I know exactly which task I'm working on.
Each workspace also maintains its own timer settings. So if I want 50-minute deep work sessions for writing and shorter 25-minute sessions for admin tasks, I just switch workspaces.

Koncentro is a cross-platform productivity app that combines a Pomodoro timer, task management, and website blocking into separate workspaces, helping you stay focused and eliminate distractions.

            Koncentro won't replace your entire workflow, but it fills a real gap
    
Koncentro isn't a flashy app with a long feature list. It doesn't have calendar integration, cloud sync, or even notifications when you overrun your task time. You can't duplicate workspaces or copy blocklists between them, and the initial proxy setup is a little rough around the edges. These are the kinds of things you'd expect from an app still in its early stages.
But for what it does, it does it well. A lightweight task manager, a system-level website blocker, and a Pomodoro timer bundled into one open-source app that runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux. If you've been piecing together separate tools to stay focused like I was, Koncentro is worth trying. It's not perfect, but it's the first productivity app that actually made me delete three others.
