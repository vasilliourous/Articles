                        What is Immich?
               

            And what does it replace?
    
If you're at all interested in self-hosting, there's a good chance you've heard of Immich before, but I'll briefly explain it anyway. It's a photo and video storage service, not unlike Google Photos. Except it's very much unlike Google Photos, since you don't have to put your data in a cloud server and trust Google with it.
Truthfully, though, I was never much of a Google Photos user. For me, Immich replaced 
OneDrive
, which is where all my personal photos and videos were stored for the longest time. Of course, it can also replace Google Photos if you're coming from there, just as it can replace other cloud storage services you may be using for your photos.
And yes, when compared to OneDrive, Immich is genuinely better.

                        A massive UI improvement
               

            OneDrive is not good
    
Compared to using OneDrive, Immich is a pretty massive step up in terms of usability. OneDrive has improved the way it handles photos over time, but loading anything on the service is still relatively slow and it can be painful to get around. There's a nice timeline now for browsing all your images at once, but it's still not ideal.
Immich has a UI that closely replicates that of Google Photos, and it feels both modern and fast in comparison. The default view shows a full timeline of every photo I have saved, and I can either scroll through it bit by bit, or I can use the scroll bar to jump to a specific point in time in an instant, which makes browsing very easy.
Of course, you can also create albums, similar to OneDrive, but this too works a bit better here, both in terms of performance and features. Things load a lot faster in Immich and they're presented in a more organized way, but there's also a slideshow feature that OneDrive somehow doesn't have. If you access your OneDrive photos through the Photos app on Windows 11, you can make a slideshow that way, but not on the web interface.
And there's even more to it. Immich also has facial recognition to identify people in photos, but again, that information never leaves your own server. Actually, there are a few AI features you can enable, like optical character recognition and duplicate detection.

                        Private, free, and fully-featured
               

            You can still have multiple user accounts
    
As I've mentioned, I'm not an avid Google Photos user, but I am familiar with it, and Immich still comes out on top for me. In terms of features and overall functionality, these two are pretty similar, and Immich borrows most of its cues from Google Photos. Google does have many editing features built-in that Immich doesn't, but I don't use these services for their editing features, I use them to upload and store the images once I've edited them to look how I want them to.
The big benefit of Immich over Google Photos is, of course, the privacy and importantly, the cost. Aside from being on your own server, Immich being self-hosted means you don't need a subscription to host more than a certain amount of data. As long as your NAS has enough space, you can add as many files as you want without paying more.
And that becomes all the more important if you have multiple people in your family. Immich lets you create multiple users, and they can all use the same disk to store their photos, keeping them separate from each other's profiles, and without any extra cost per user, or without having to create multiple deployments of the app. A single Immich server can have as many user accounts as needed, as long as the storage device has space.
